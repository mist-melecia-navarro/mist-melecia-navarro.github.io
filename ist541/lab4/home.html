Welcome

An Intern's Guide to Designing & Conducting Client-Centered Therapy Sessions in Field Practice

You’ve made it to the internship phase… (calmly). YOU MADE IT TO THE INTERNSHIP PHASE! AHHHHH!

Buckle up! Because you are headed out of the classroom and straight to reality! Real clients, real sessions, zero prep! Or, at least, that is how it may feel. You were selected for a therapy internship, but you have a sinking question...

What am I supposed to actually do in a therapy session?"

Entering a therapy internship can feel like stepping into a high-stakes environment with limited preparation. Therapy is a complex process with layers of nuance; clients grapple with a wide variety of symptoms and issues, stemming from a variety of causes that can be addressed with different techniques. With all the variables...

How, then, might an incoming intern prepare for conducting a therapy session?

As a Master of Social Work student,I experienced firsthand the gap between classroom knowledge and application of skills in the field. Assigned to a therapy placement in my first year, I remember scrambling to feel prepared—rushing to the bookstore to buy therapy books, reading modality summaries, and cobbling together my first session outline based on instinct and scattered advice. Conversations with peers confirmed I wasn’t alone. Many of us felt overwhelmed by the ambiguity of how to "do" therapy.

This project is driven by that experience—and a desire to change it.

The Therapy Crash Course: An Intern’s Guide to Designing and Conducting Client-Centered Therapy Sessions in Field Practice isn’t about creating perfect therapists on day one. It’s about providing interns with structure, support, and confidence with just enough scaffolding so they don’t enter the room to conduct their first few sessions empty-handed.

This short course introduces therapy basics, centered around these questions:

  • What is Therapy, Anyway?
  • Where Shall We Begin?
  • What Do I Say?
  • Now What?

The internship phase is a time of incredible growth as you put into practice your natural talent, your years of experience working with people, and your growing MSW knowledge to work! This is what you came to grad school for... to level up in your path!

I hope you leave feeling more prepared, confident, and cared for!
