Modules 1-4
M1: What is Therapy, Anyway? THERAPY & THERAPISTS

Research keeps reminding us that you—the person of the therapist—matter a lot. Decades of work show that the early working alliance—shared goals, shared tasks, and a real bond—predicts outcomes across approaches; and even a single session or the first few hours can create durable relief. Clients’ confidence in you and in the treatment plan often moves with their week‑to‑week progress.

Open Module 1
M2: Where Shall We Begin? STAGES OF CHANGE

The classic social work phrase of "meeting them where they are at" applies here! We've all been in a journey of change at some point, and without the right starting point, we are just hoping for the best! Ensure the greatest chance of success by identifying where the client is in the stages of change. Alignment is key in what goal is selected and the corresponding treatment plan. You'll feel a sense of transcendence when you can recognize the stages in clients!

Open Module 2
M3: What Do I Say? Motivational Interviewing — Draw out their own reasons to change.

"Don't take all the good lines." Traditional approaches tend to center the practitioner as the advocate for change. It feels like you are pulling out all the tricks to convince the client with arguments for why and how. Module 3 helps answer that question of what is to be said in the therapy session, and in fact, it isn't about what we say but how we draw out the client's own motivations.

Open Module 3
M4: Now What? Collaborative Goal Setting — Turn motivation into doable next steps.

 It all comes back to the goal! Creating a goal in therapy is an art, it is a fluid mix of structure and flexibility. And when things get tough, your colleagues will always remind you, "what is your goal?" A goal is the center of the work, where the beginning phases lead to and where we strive to get to. Make it collaborative with the client! Shrink the gap between wanting and doing. Co-create a plan that removes barriers, builds skills, and taps outside supports.
